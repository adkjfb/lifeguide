Eurasian Hoopoe

Lifespan: 10 years

Weight: 50-80 grams

Diet: Mostly insect larvae, also small reptiles and frogs

Predators: Snakes, including western whip snakes, European asps, and black rat snakes, birds, including little owls and common magpies, and red foxes

Range: Europe, Asia, Northern Africa

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: Female Eurasian hoopoes put secretions from the uropygial gland onto their eggs to protect the embryos from infection. Beneficial bacteria in the uropygial gland produces an antiseptic that protect the eggs from feather eating bacteria. Adult birds also use this gland to preen their feathers, preventing feather degredation.


Fun Fact: Eurasian hoopoes exhibit hatching asynchrony, meaning the eggs hatch at different times. The purpose of hatching asynchrony is uncertain, but soem scientists believe it is to give the earlier hatchers a head start and increase their chances of survival. Another theory is that it reduces the risk of predation, as it shrinks the time that all the hatchlings are in the nest together.
