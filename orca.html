Orca

Lifespan: 50-90 years

Top speed: 35 mph

Weight: 6,600-8,800 pounds for females, 8,000-12,000 for males

Diet: Fish, cephalopods, seals, sharks, dolphins, baleen whales

Predators: No natural predators

Range: All oceans

Conservation Status: Data Deficient

Fun Fact: Orcas will intentionally beach themselves to hunt prey that is more vulnerable on land, such as seals and sea lions.


Fun Fact: Orcas sometimes use a technique called "wave washing" while hunting to knock seals off ice flows. They swim in a synchronized formation toward the ice to create a wave, which knocks the seal into the water where it can be caught more easily.
