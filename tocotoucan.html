Toco Toucan

Lifespan: Up to 20 years

Top speed: 40 mph

Weight: 20 ozs

Diet: Primarily fruit, also small vertebrates, eggs, and insects

Predators: Jaguars, eagles, boas, margays, and owls

Range: Central and Eastern South America

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: A toucans bill is almost as long as its body. It is hollow and used for feeding, attracting mates, and releasing heat.


Fun Fact: Toco toucans are the largest kind of toucan. It is also the basalmost member of the genus ramphastos, meaning it is least related to the other members of its genus.
