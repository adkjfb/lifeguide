Tiger Shark

Lifespan: Typically 20-30 years, though they can live as long as 50 years

Top speed: Estimated to be around 20-25 mph

Weight: Generally 800-1,400 lbs but one tiger shark was found in 1957 that was over 3,000 lbs

Diet: One of the widest diets of any shark, including fish, seals, squids, birds, sea turtles, and crustaceans

Predators: Orcas

Range: Temperate and tropical oceans and seas, except the Mediterranean sea

Conservation Status: Near Threatened

Fun Fact: Like all members of the chondrichthyes class, tiger sharks have ampullae of Lorenzini, which are mucus-filled pores that allow them to sense electrical fields created by movement in prey's muscles.


Fun Fact: Tiger sharks will eat almost anything, and have been found with license plates and tires in their stomaches.
