Marbled Headstander

Lifespan: Up to 10 years

Weight: 80-150 grams

Diet: Crustaceans, insects, worms, and plant matter

Predators: Larger fish, including catfish and large cichlids, wading or fish eating birds like herons, aquatic reptiles

Range: Amazon river basin, South American freshwater rivers

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: Marbled headstanders get their name from the way they swim head-down to graze algae and plant matter from the bottoms of rivers.


Fun Fact: Juvenile marbled headstanders form schools for protection, but adults are solitary.
